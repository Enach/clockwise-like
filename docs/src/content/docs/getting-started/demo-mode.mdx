Not ready to connect your calendar yet? Demo mode lets you explore every part of Paceday using sample data so you can get a feel for the product before linking your real account.

## How to enter demo mode

You can enter demo mode in two ways:

- **From the login page** — click **Try a demo** below the sign-in buttons. No account required.
- **Automatically** — if Paceday cannot reach the server when you open the app (for example, during a network outage or maintenance window), it drops into demo mode on its own and shows a banner so you know you're looking at sample data.

[Screenshot: Login page with the "Try a demo" link highlighted below the Google and Microsoft sign-in buttons]

## What you'll see in demo mode

Demo mode populates Paceday with a realistic week of sample events, focus blocks, habits, and — if you switch to Manager profile — a fictional team view. Everything looks and works like the real product. You can click around, open events, tap into habit trackers, and explore the meeting prep brief for a sample meeting.

## What demo mode cannot do

Demo mode has a few intentional limitations:

- **No real calendar sync.** Any events you see are fictional. Nothing is read from or written to a real calendar.
- **Writes stay local.** If you create or edit an event, it appears in the demo but disappears as soon as you refresh or close the browser tab. Nothing is saved.
- **No integrations.** Slack and Notion connections are shown as placeholders — they cannot be activated until you sign in with a real account.
- **Demo banner always visible.** A yellow bar at the top of the screen reminds you that you're in demo mode so there's no confusion about whether your real calendar is being affected.

[Screenshot: Demo mode banner at the top of the screen reading "You're in demo mode — changes won't be saved"]

## When you're ready

Click **Connect your calendar** in the demo banner or in the sidebar to leave demo mode and link your real Google or Microsoft account. Any sample data is discarded — your real calendar takes over cleanly.
