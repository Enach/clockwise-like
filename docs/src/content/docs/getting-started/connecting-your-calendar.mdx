---
title: Connecting your calendar
description: How to connect Google Calendar or Microsoft Outlook to Paceday.
---

Paceday works with your existing calendar — no data migration, no duplicate events. The connection takes about a minute and goes through your provider's standard sign-in screen, so your credentials never touch Paceday's servers.

## Connect Google Calendar

1. Open Paceday and click **Connect your calendar** on the welcome screen (or go to **Settings > Calendar** if you already have an account).
2. Click **Sign in with Google**.
3. Choose the Google account that holds your work calendar.
4. You'll see a Google permissions dialog. Click **Allow** to let Paceday read your calendar events and free/busy information.
5. You'll be taken back to Paceday — your events will start appearing within a few seconds.

## Connect Microsoft Outlook

1. Open Paceday and click **Connect your calendar** on the welcome screen (or go to **Settings > Calendar**).
2. Click **Sign in with Microsoft**.
3. Sign in with your work Microsoft account (the one managed by your company's IT).
4. Microsoft will ask you to confirm the permissions. Click **Accept**.
5. You'll land back in Paceday with your calendar loaded.

[Screenshot: Microsoft permissions dialog showing the Accept button]

## What Paceday reads — and what it never touches

Paceday only ever looks at your **calendar events and free/busy availability**. That's it.

Paceday never reads, stores, or sees:

- Your emails or email attachments
- Your contacts or address book
- Slack messages or Teams chats (unless you separately connect those integrations)
- Files in Google Drive or OneDrive

Your calendar data is used only to schedule focus blocks, display your week, and — for managers — populate the team view. It is never sold or shared with third parties.

## What to do if the connection fails

If you hit an error during sign-in, it's usually one of three things: a browser pop-up blocker preventing the OAuth window, a company IT policy restricting third-party apps, or simply needing to try a different browser. See the troubleshooting guide for step-by-step fixes.
