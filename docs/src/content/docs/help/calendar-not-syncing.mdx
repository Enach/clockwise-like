title: Calendar not syncing
description: Fix common calendar sync issues in Paceday, including expired OAuth tokens, revoked permissions, and initial sync problems.

If your calendar events aren't showing up in Paceday, or if changes you make in Google Calendar or Outlook aren't reflected in Paceday, there are a few common causes. Most of them are quick to fix.

## Common reasons your calendar might not be syncing

**Expired OAuth token** — Your authorization with Google or Microsoft has a time limit. If it expires, Paceday loses the ability to read your calendar until you reconnect. This happens automatically over time or if you change your account password.

**Calendar permissions revoked** — If you visited your Google Account settings or Microsoft account settings and removed Paceday's access, the connection breaks immediately.

**Network issue during initial sync** — If something went wrong when you first connected your calendar (a slow connection, a timeout, an interrupted page load), the initial pull of your events may be incomplete.

## Step-by-step fix

**Step 1: Reconnect your calendar**
1. Go to **Settings > Integrations**.
2. Find your calendar provider — Google Calendar or Microsoft Outlook.
3. Click **Reconnect** next to it.
4. Sign in to your Google or Microsoft account and approve the permissions again.
5. Wait about 30 seconds, then refresh Paceday.

This resolves the vast majority of sync issues.

**Step 2: Sign out and sign back in**

If reconnecting doesn't help:
1. Click your avatar or initials in the top-right corner of Paceday.
2. Click **Sign out**.
3. Sign back in with the same Google or Microsoft account you normally use.

Signing back in triggers a fresh sync of your calendar data.

**Step 3: Contact support**

If your calendar is still not syncing after both steps above, reach out to the Paceday support team. [Contact support](#) and include:
- Which calendar provider you use (Google or Microsoft)
- Roughly when the sync stopped working
- Any error messages you saw
