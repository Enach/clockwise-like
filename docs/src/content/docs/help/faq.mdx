title: Frequently asked questions
description: Quick answers to the most common questions about Paceday — privacy, data, supported calendar providers, and platform availability.
---

## Does Paceday read my emails?

No. Paceday does not access your Gmail inbox or Outlook email at any point. Paceday reads your **calendar events** (titles, times, attendees, and free/busy status). If you connect Slack, it also reads messages in channels you're a member of. If you connect Notion, it reads pages in your workspace. That's the full scope.

## Can my manager see my event titles?

No. Managers who use Paceday can see **aggregate minutes only** — your total focus time and total meeting time for the week, plus a week-over-week trend. They never see the names, descriptions, or attendees of individual events. This is enforced at the data level, not just a display setting. You can also turn off analytics sharing entirely in **Settings > Profile**.

## What happens to my data if I cancel my Paceday account?

Your calendar events remain exactly where they are — in Google Calendar or Microsoft Outlook. Paceday doesn't own your events; it only reads and writes to your calendar on your behalf. When you delete your Paceday account, Paceday permanently deletes all stored data associated with your account within **30 days**. This includes any cached calendar data, Slack context, and Notion content held in Paceday's systems.

## Does Paceday work with Apple Calendar?

Not yet. Paceday currently supports **Google Calendar** and **Microsoft Outlook / Microsoft 365**. Apple Calendar (iCloud) is not supported at this time. If you use Apple Calendar alongside a Google or Microsoft account, you can still connect the Google or Microsoft calendar to Paceday — but iCloud events won't be visible.

## Is there a mobile app?

Not yet. Paceday is a **web app** designed for desktop browsers. It works well in Chrome, Firefox, Edge, and Safari on a laptop or desktop. A mobile app is on the roadmap, but there's no release date to share yet. In the meantime, Paceday's Slack integration means your daily recap reaches you on mobile even if the main app is desktop-only.

## Is my free/busy data shared with other users?

Your free/busy information is only shared **within your organization** — specifically, within the same Google Workspace or Microsoft 365 tenant. It's used for scheduling purposes only (for example, when a colleague or manager uses Find a time). Paceday does not share your availability with users outside your organization, and it does not sell or share any data with third parties.
