title: Analytics privacy settings

If your manager uses Paceday and has Manager mode enabled, they have the option to view focus and meeting time data for their team. Whether they can see your data depends on a setting you control yourself.

## What the "Share analytics with manager" setting does

When this setting is turned on, your manager can see:
- Your total **focus minutes** for the current week
- Your total **meeting minutes** for the current week
- A **week-over-week trend** (up or down, with a percentage)
- A **12-week chart** of your focus time history

That's it. They see aggregated totals — not event titles, not attendee names, not descriptions, not anything about specific meetings or tasks.

When this setting is turned off, your row in your manager's team view shows as unavailable. They won't see any data from you.

## What your manager never sees

Regardless of this setting, your manager cannot see:
- Individual event titles or descriptions
- Who you're meeting with
- The content of your Slack messages or Notion pages
- Your personal habits or scheduling links

This is enforced at the data level — the aggregate view is all that's ever shared.

## How to turn it off

1. Go to **Settings > Profile**.
2. Scroll down to the **Privacy** section.
3. Uncheck **Share focus analytics with my manager**.
4. Click **Save**.

The change takes effect immediately. Your manager's view will stop showing your data the next time they refresh their team panel.

## Default behavior

This setting is **on by default** when you create your Paceday account. The idea is that most teams benefit from a lightweight shared view of work health, and the data shared is intentionally minimal. But you're always free to opt out, no questions asked.
