The Analytics dashboard gives you an honest picture of where your working hours actually go. Open it by clicking **Analytics** in the left sidebar. You'll see a snapshot of the current week alongside a longer-term view so you can spot trends, not just today's firefighting.

[Screenshot: The full Analytics dashboard showing the donut chart, metric cards, and 8-week bar chart]

## Your four key metrics

**Focus time** — the total number of hours this week where you had uninterrupted, meeting-free blocks of at least 45 minutes. This is the number to grow.

**Meeting time** — the total scheduled meeting hours this week, counting from your connected calendar. Recurring events, one-offs, and external calls all count.

**Habits completion rate** — the percentage of your scheduled habits that were either completed or skipped intentionally this week. Habits missed because a meeting displaced them and couldn't be rescheduled lower this number.

**Focus score** — a 0–100 score that reflects the ratio of focus time to meeting time. A score above 60 generally means your calendar is healthy. Below 40 is a signal that meetings are crowding out deep work time.

## The donut chart

The donut chart in the centre of the dashboard shows **this week's time split** at a glance: focus time in blue, meeting time in orange, and unaccounted time (evenings, weekends, or gaps too short to be focus blocks) in light grey. Hover over any segment to see the exact hours.

## The 8-week bar chart

Below the donut you'll find a bar chart covering the **past 8 weeks**. Each bar is split into the same colours as the donut. This view helps you see whether your focus time is trending up or down over time — one bad week is noise, but four bad weeks in a row is a pattern worth addressing.

## Meeting cost

At the bottom of the dashboard you'll find a **Meeting cost** field. Enter your approximate hourly rate and Paceday will multiply it by your meeting hours to give you an estimated dollar cost of the meetings on your calendar this week. This is optional and completely private — the number is only ever shown to you.
