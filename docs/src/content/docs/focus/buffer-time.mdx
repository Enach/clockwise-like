Buffer time is a small gap — typically 5 to 15 minutes — that Paceday automatically adds before and after your meetings. It gives you a moment to glance at your notes before a call, or to close out your thoughts and get a glass of water once it ends. Small gaps, big difference.

## How to enable buffer time

1. Go to **Settings** in the left sidebar.
2. Open the **Focus time** tab.
3. Toggle **Buffer time** on.

[Screenshot: The buffer time section inside Settings > Focus time, showing the toggle and duration fields]

## Configuring before and after durations

Once buffer time is enabled, you'll see two fields:

- **Before meetings** — how many minutes to reserve before each meeting starts (e.g., 10 minutes).
- **After meetings** — how many minutes to reserve after each meeting ends (e.g., 5 minutes).

You can set these independently. For example, you might want 10 minutes to prepare before a meeting but only 5 minutes to decompress afterward. Tap the number field and type your preferred duration, then click **Save**.

## Skip back-to-back meetings

The **Skip back-to-back meetings** toggle controls what happens when two meetings sit right next to each other on your calendar — for example, one ending at 2:00 PM and the next starting at 2:00 PM.

When this option is **on**, Paceday does not try to wedge a buffer between them. There simply isn't space, and adding one would shift your next meeting, which Paceday will never do. Turning it on avoids creating misleading buffer blocks that can't actually be used.

When it's **off**, Paceday adds the buffer block anyway, but it will overlap with the adjacent meeting — useful if you want the visual reminder even when the time isn't truly free.

Most people leave this toggled **on**.

## How buffers look on the calendar

Buffer blocks appear in **light grey** on your calendar. They are not clickable or editable — they exist purely to mark protected breathing room. You cannot invite people to them, add tasks, or reschedule them manually. If you cancel the meeting they belong to, the buffer disappears automatically.
