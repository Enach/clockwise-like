---
title: What is Focus Time?
description: Learn how Paceday identifies and protects blocks of uninterrupted time so you can do your best work.
---

Focus time is any stretch of your day where no meetings are scheduled — time that belongs entirely to you and the work that actually requires your full attention. Paceday automatically identifies these blocks and treats them as something worth protecting.

## Why it matters

Every time a meeting interrupts deep work, your brain needs roughly 20 minutes to get back into flow. That means a single poorly placed 30-minute meeting can effectively wipe out an hour of productive time on either side of it. Over a week, fragmented calendars quietly steal hours you never get back.

## How Paceday finds your focus blocks

Paceday scans your calendar continuously and flags any gap between meetings that is at least 45 minutes long. Shorter gaps — say, a 15-minute window between two calls — aren't marked as focus time because they're rarely long enough to do meaningful work.

When Paceday finds a qualifying gap, it:

1. Marks the block in **blue** on your calendar so it stands out at a glance.
2. Counts it toward your weekly focus time total in the Analytics dashboard.
3. Uses it when scheduling habits, so your recurring personal events land inside these open windows whenever possible.

[Screenshot: Calendar view showing a 90-minute blue focus block between two meetings]

## What Paceday does not do

Focus time blocks are informational — they show you what's available, but Paceday does not automatically decline meeting invites or lock those slots. If a colleague books a meeting during a focus block, it simply gets recalculated once the invite lands on your calendar.

To actively defend focus time, use habits (recurring personal events that hold a slot) or configure buffer time around your meetings.
