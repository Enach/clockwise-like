Walking into a meeting without context is a productivity tax. Meeting prep briefs solve that by pulling together the relevant Slack conversations, Notion pages, and likely goals for a meeting — automatically, before you need them.

## What is a meeting prep brief?

A brief is a short, AI-generated summary that appears inside Paceday before a meeting you're organizing. It gathers signals from your connected tools and surfaces the things most likely to matter in that conversation.

Briefs only appear when:

- You are the **organizer** of the meeting (not just an attendee)
- You have **Slack and/or Notion connected** to Paceday
- The meeting is on your calendar within the **next 24 hours**

## Reading your brief

Each brief has three sections:

### Documents to review

Notion pages that are directly related to the meeting, based on the event title, attendees, and recent activity. If a page was edited by an attendee in the last week or shares keywords with the meeting topic, it surfaces here. Click any document to open it in Notion.

### Recent conversations

A summary of Slack messages involving the meeting attendees over the past few days, filtered to threads that seem topically related to the meeting. This helps you catch decisions or open questions that happened outside of email.

### Probable goals

A short list of inferred objectives for the meeting, generated from the event title, description, and recent context. Think of this as a quick sanity check — it's not a formal agenda, just a prompt to help you walk in with the right mindset.

## The 24-hour cache

Briefs are generated once per day and then cached. This keeps the experience fast and avoids repeatedly hitting connected tools. The brief you see is no older than 24 hours.

If something important has happened since the brief was generated — a last-minute Slack thread, a Notion page update — you can refresh manually by clicking the **Refresh brief** button at the top of the brief panel. This pulls the latest data and regenerates the summary on the spot.
