The Paceday calendar is the central place where your whole week comes together — meetings, focus time, habits, and breathing room all in one view. Here's how to get your bearings.

## Week view and day view

When you open Paceday, you land on the **week view** by default. This shows Monday through Friday (or Sunday through Saturday, depending on your locale setting) as columns, with time slots running down the page.

To zoom in on a single day, click the day's name at the top of its column. This opens the **day view**, which gives you a larger, more detailed look at that day's events and any gaps between them.

To go back to the week view, click **Week** in the top-left navigation bar.

[Screenshot: Paceday calendar in week view, showing color-coded events across five days]

## Navigating between weeks

- Click the **left arrow** next to the date range to go back one week.
- Click the **right arrow** to move forward one week.
- Click **Today** to jump back to the current week at any time.

You can also click directly on a date in the mini calendar in the left sidebar — this jumps the main view to the week containing that date.

## Color coding

Every event type has its own color so you can read your week at a glance:

| Color | What it means |
|-------|---------------|
| Yellow | **Meeting** — a scheduled event with other people |
| Blue | **Focus block** — time Paceday has protected for deep work |
| Purple | **Habit** — a recurring commitment you're tracking in Paceday |
| Grey | **Buffer gap** — a short breathing space Paceday leaves between back-to-back events |

If an event is faded or shown with a diagonal pattern, it means the event was declined or is tentative.

[Screenshot: Close-up of a single day showing a yellow meeting, a blue focus block, and a purple habit event stacked vertically]

## Clicking on an event

Click any event to open a small summary panel. This shows the event title, time, attendees (for meetings), and any notes. For meetings you organize, you'll also see a **View brief** button if a meeting prep brief is available.
