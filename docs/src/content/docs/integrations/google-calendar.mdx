Google Calendar is Paceday's primary calendar provider. When you sign up with a Google account, Paceday connects to your calendar to read your schedule, protect your focus time, and help you book meetings — all from one place.

## What permissions Paceday requests

During sign-in, Google shows you a permission screen listing what Paceday is asking for. Here's what each permission means and why Paceday needs it:

**Read your calendar events**
Paceday reads your existing events to understand your schedule, generate meeting briefs, find focus time, and detect 1:1 patterns if you're in manager mode.

**Create and edit events**
Paceday creates events on your behalf when you schedule habits, use the Find a time tool, or book through a scheduling link. It also edits events to add buffer time or update focus blocks.

**Read free/busy information**
This lets Paceday check availability for your team members and colleagues — the same information Google Calendar shows when you invite someone to a meeting.

## What Paceday never accesses

Paceday's permissions are limited to your calendar. It does not access:
- Your Gmail messages or email content
- Your Google Contacts
- Google Drive files or documents
- Any other Google services

If you're ever unsure, you can review and revoke Paceday's access at any time through your [Google Account security settings](https://myaccount.google.com/permissions).

## Reconnect if access is revoked

If you revoked access or your Google session expired, Paceday will show a banner letting you know your calendar isn't syncing.

To reconnect:
1. Go to **Settings > Integrations**.
2. Find the **Google Calendar** card and click **Reconnect**.
3. Sign in with your Google account and approve the permissions again.

If reconnecting doesn't resolve the issue, try signing out of Paceday completely and signing back in with your Google account.
