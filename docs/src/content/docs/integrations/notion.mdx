title: Notion integration
description: Connect Notion so Paceday can surface relevant pages in your meeting briefs, helping you walk into every meeting prepared.
---

When you connect Notion, Paceday automatically finds pages related to your upcoming meetings and surfaces them in your meeting briefs under a **Documents to review** section. No more hunting through Notion right before a call — the right pages come to you.

## Connect Notion

1. Go to **Settings > Integrations**.
2. Find the **Notion** card and click **Connect**.
3. You'll be redirected to Notion's authorization page. Sign in if needed.
4. Select which Notion workspaces or pages Paceday should have access to, then click **Allow access**.
5. Once redirected back to Paceday, your connection is active.

[Screenshot: Notion authorization screen showing workspace selection]

## What Paceday reads from Notion

Paceday scans your Notion workspace for pages that are relevant to your meetings. It looks at:

- Pages from the **last 90 days**
- Pages whose titles or content match **your meeting titles and attendee names**

Paceday does not read every page in your workspace — only those that appear relevant to an upcoming meeting. It does not write to Notion or create pages on your behalf.

## How Notion results appear in meeting briefs

Open any upcoming meeting in Paceday's event panel. If Paceday found relevant Notion pages, you'll see a **Documents to review** section listing those pages with their titles and a direct link. Click any link to open the page directly in Notion.

If no relevant pages are found, the section won't appear — Paceday only shows it when there's something useful to surface.

## Disconnect Notion

1. Go to **Settings > Integrations**.
2. Click the **Notion** card.
3. Click **Disconnect**.

Disconnecting removes Paceday's access to your Notion workspace. The Documents to review section will no longer appear in briefs. You can reconnect at any time without losing any settings.
