Setting up a habit takes about two minutes. Once it's saved, Paceday starts scheduling it automatically around your meetings — no further effort needed.

## Step by step

1. Click **Habits** in the left sidebar to open the Habits section.
2. Click the **+** button in the top-right corner, or choose one of the starter templates if you'd like a head start.
3. Fill in the fields described below.
4. Click **Save habit**.

[Screenshot: The habit creation form showing the title, duration, days, time window, priority, and color fields]

## Fields explained

**Title** — Give your habit a name that you'll recognise on the calendar, like "Morning run" or "Deep work."

**Duration** — How long the habit needs. Pick anything from 15 minutes to 4 hours.

**Which days** — Select the days of the week this habit should repeat. You can pick any combination — it does not have to be every weekday.

**Time window** — The window is the range of hours within which Paceday is allowed to schedule the habit. For example, setting a window of 07:00–09:00 means Paceday will only place the habit in that two-hour slot. If no space is available inside the window, the habit is marked missed for that day.

**Priority** — When you have multiple habits competing for the same open time, priority determines who goes first. Habits with a higher priority get scheduled before lower-priority ones. Think of it as a queue — the most important habit claims its spot, and the rest fill in around it.

**Color** — Choose a colour so the habit stands out on your calendar at a glance.

## Starter templates

Not sure where to begin? Paceday includes 12 ready-made templates you can use as-is or tweak to fit your routine:

Morning workout, Deep work block, Reading time, Meditation, Language learning, Journaling, Team standup prep, Inbox zero, Walking break, Creative time, Learning & development, and Weekly review.

Tap any template to pre-fill the form, then adjust the duration, window, or days to match your schedule.
