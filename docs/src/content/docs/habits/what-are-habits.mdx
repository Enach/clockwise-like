---
title: What Are Habits?
description: Habits are recurring personal events that Paceday automatically reschedules around your meetings so they actually happen.
---

A habit is a recurring personal event — like a morning workout, a daily reading block, or a deep work session — that you want to protect on your calendar every day. You tell Paceday what you want to do, when you'd like to do it, and how long it takes, and Paceday finds a spot for it automatically.

## How habits differ from regular calendar events

When you create a normal calendar event, it sits at a fixed time. If a meeting lands on top of it, the event gets buried and usually forgotten.

Habits work differently. If a meeting conflicts with a scheduled habit, Paceday does not leave it buried — it looks for another open window within the same day and reschedules the habit there automatically. You don't have to drag anything around yourself.

This makes habits much more resilient than blocked calendar time. They move with your day instead of getting crushed by it.

## A few examples

- **Morning workout** — 45 minutes, scheduled between 07:00 and 09:00, Monday through Friday.
- **Deep work block** — 90 minutes reserved for heads-down project work, any time before noon.
- **Reading time** — 30 minutes in the evening, every weekday.

All three would survive a busy meeting day because Paceday keeps trying to find them a home.

## What happens if no window is available?

If your day is genuinely fully booked and there is no open slot that fits the habit's duration, Paceday marks it as **missed** for that day. A missed day due to a completely blocked calendar does not count against your streak. You can read more about how streaks and completion work in the link below.
