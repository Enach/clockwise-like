---
title: Streaks and Completion
description: See how Paceday tracks consecutive habit completions, what counts as a miss, and how displaced habits are handled.
---

Every habit has a streak — a count of consecutive days where it was completed. Streaks are a simple way to see whether a habit is actually sticking, and they reset to zero if you miss a day that was within your control.

## Marking a habit done or skipping it

When a habit appears on your calendar, you'll see a small popover when you click on it. From there you can:

- Click **Done** to mark it as completed for that day. Your streak increases by one.
- Click **Skip** to log the habit as intentionally skipped. Skipped days do not break your streak — they're treated as planned rest, not failure.

You can also mark a habit done directly from the Habits section in the sidebar, where you'll see all of today's habits listed with a quick-complete button next to each one.

## What happens when a meeting displaces a habit

If a meeting lands on your scheduled habit time, Paceday doesn't give up on the habit. Instead, it immediately looks for another open window within the same day that fits inside your configured time window.

Here's the order of events:

1. A meeting is added to your calendar that overlaps with a habit.
2. Paceday searches for another available slot within your habit's time window on the same day.
3. If a slot is found, the habit moves there automatically. You'll see it shift on the calendar.
4. If no slot is available within the window, the habit is marked **missed** for that day.

## Missed habits and your streak

A missed habit normally breaks your streak. However, there is one exception: if your calendar was **fully blocked** that day — meaning there was genuinely no available window within your habit's time window regardless of where Paceday looked — the missed day does not count against your streak.

This prevents you from being penalized on days where you had back-to-back external meetings with no gaps. Paceday recognises the difference between a habit you forgot and a day that was simply unavoidable.
