title: 1:1 cadences
description: Set the expected meeting frequency for each team member and get alerted when a 1:1 is overdue so you never let an important check-in slip.
---

A 1:1 cadence is the rhythm you want to keep with each person on your team — how often you expect to meet with them one-on-one. Paceday tracks this for you and flags when a gap is growing too wide, so you can stay consistent without keeping it all in your head.

## Set a cadence for each team member

1. Open **My Team** in the sidebar.
2. Click on a team member's name to open their panel.
3. Find the **1:1 Cadence** section and click the frequency dropdown.
4. Choose one of the preset options:
   - **Weekly**
   - **Every 2 weeks**
   - **Monthly**
   - **Custom** — enter a number of days that works for your relationship
5. Paceday saves your selection automatically.

You can set a different cadence for each person. For example, you might check in with a newer team member weekly while meeting a more senior colleague every two weeks.

## The "Upcoming 1:1 gaps" alert

Paceday watches your calendar and compares your actual meeting history against the cadence you've set. If you haven't had a 1:1 with someone recently — and there's no upcoming one booked either — you'll see an **Upcoming 1:1 gap** alert next to their name in your **My Team** list.

The alert appears when:
- Your last 1:1 with them is older than your cadence window, **and**
- There's no 1:1 scheduled within the next cadence window.

This is not a notification that fires at a specific time — it's a live indicator in the My Team list that you'll see whenever you open the sidebar.

## Schedule a 1:1 with one click

Next to each gap alert, you'll see a **Schedule** button. Tap it and Paceday opens the new event drawer pre-filled with:

- The team member's name in the event title
- Their email address in the guest field
- Your default meeting duration

From there, just pick a time, adjust anything you'd like, and send the invite. Paceday handles the rest.
