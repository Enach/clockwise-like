Instead of scanning everyone's calendars yourself, use Paceday's **Find a time** tool to surface the best open slots for your whole team at once. It factors in your no-meeting zones, each person's focus blocks, and overall calendar density — and then ranks the results so the best option is obvious.

## How to find a slot

1. Open **My Team** in the sidebar and go to the **Find a time** tab.
2. Choose a **date range** — for example, "This week" or a custom range of a few days.
3. Set the **duration** for the meeting (15 min, 30 min, 1 hour, etc.).
4. Click **Find slots**.

Paceday scans everyone's calendars and returns a ranked list of available windows in a few seconds.

## Reading the results

Each suggested slot has a **quality indicator** — a colored dot that tells you at a glance how clean the slot is:

- **Green dot** — everyone on your team is confirmed free during this window. This is the ideal pick.
- **Yellow dot** — one or more team members have some uncertainty (a tentative event, a free/busy status Paceday can't fully verify). The slot is likely workable, but worth double-checking.
- **Grey dot** — there's a known conflict for at least one team member. Paceday still shows these in case you need to make a judgment call, but they're deprioritized.

The top result also often carries a **Recommended** badge. This is the slot that best balances everyone's availability, avoids no-meeting zones, and falls during reasonable working hours.

## Scheduling from the results

When you've found a slot you like:

1. Click **Schedule meeting** next to that slot.
2. The event drawer opens, pre-filled with the time, duration, and your team members as guests.
3. Add a title, adjust anything you'd like, and send the invite.

The event is created directly in your calendar — no copy-pasting or tab-switching needed.
