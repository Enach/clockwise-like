---
title: Protected hours (no-meeting zones)
description: Create shared blocks of time when your whole team should be meeting-free, and let Paceday automatically exclude those windows when finding scheduling slots.
---

Protected hours — also called no-meeting zones — are recurring time blocks you reserve for your team's focused work. When you set one up, Paceday treats those windows as off-limits for meetings and excludes them when suggesting times to schedule.

Think of it like agreeing as a team that "Friday afternoons are for heads-down work." Once you define that in Paceday, the whole scheduling experience respects it automatically.

## How to create a no-meeting zone

1. Open **My Team** in the sidebar.
2. Go to the **No-meeting zones** tab at the top of the team panel.
3. Click **Add zone**.
4. Pick the **days of the week** you want to protect (you can select multiple days).
5. Set the **start time** and **end time** for the zone.
6. Give the zone a short name if you'd like — for example, "Deep work mornings" or "No meetings Friday PM."
7. Click **Save**.

You can create multiple zones if your team has more than one protected window.

[Screenshot: No-meeting zones panel with one zone already set for Friday 1 PM – 5 PM]

## How zones appear for team members

Once you save a no-meeting zone, team members who use Paceday will see it reflected in their own calendar view as a shaded, protected block. They can't edit or delete zones you've set — only you, as the manager, can manage them.

Team members who don't use Paceday won't see the zone in any special way, but the Find a time tool will still exclude those windows when suggesting slots.

## How no-meeting zones affect Find a time

When you open the **Find a time** tool and search for a slot that works for the whole team, Paceday automatically skips any windows covered by a no-meeting zone. Those times won't appear as suggestions, even if everyone technically has an open calendar at that hour.

This means you can set up your team's boundaries once and not have to remember them every time you need to book something.
