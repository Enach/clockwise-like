Paceday gives you a lightweight view into your team's work health — specifically how much focus time each person has in a given week and whether that's trending up or down. This helps you spot if someone's calendar is becoming too fragmented before it becomes a problem.

## What you'll see for each team member

In your **My Team** list, every team member shows a quick summary:

- **Focus time this week** — the total number of hours of uninterrupted focus time they have (or had) in the current week.
- **Trend indicator** — an up or down arrow with a percentage showing how this week compares to last week. A green up arrow means more focus time; a red down arrow means less.

These numbers update daily so you're always looking at a current picture.

## Paceday users vs. non-Paceday users

What Paceday can show depends on whether your team member uses Paceday themselves.

**Team members who use Paceday** share aggregated data with their manager. You'll see:
- Total focus minutes for the week
- Total meeting minutes for the week
- The weekly trend

They control this sharing in their own settings. If they turn it off, their row will show as unavailable.

**Team members who don't use Paceday** are visible too, but with less detail. Paceday can only read their free/busy information from your shared calendar system (Google Workspace or Microsoft 365). You'll see when they appear to be available or in meetings, but not their focus time breakdown.

## The 12-week chart

Click on any team member's name to open their expanded panel. At the bottom of that panel, you'll find a **12-week chart** showing their weekly focus time over the past three months. This makes it easy to see longer patterns — for example, if focus time has been declining steadily since a reorg, or if a particular week was unusually fragmented.

## A note on privacy

Paceday is designed so that managers can support their teams without feeling like surveillance. You'll always see totals — focus minutes, meeting minutes — but never the titles, descriptions, or attendees of individual events. Your team members can also choose to opt out of sharing analytics at any time in their own profile settings.
