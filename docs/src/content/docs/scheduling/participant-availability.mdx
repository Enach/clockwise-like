---
title: Participant Availability
description: Find out when Paceday can check whether meeting participants are free, and what to expect when it can't.
---

When someone books a slot through your scheduling link, Paceday can sometimes verify in advance whether other people you plan to invite are actually free during that time. This helps you avoid booking a meeting only to discover a key participant already has a conflict.

## When availability checking works

Paceday can check availability for participants who belong to the **same Google Workspace or Microsoft 365 organisation** as you. Within a shared organisation, calendar systems allow apps to query free/busy status — not the actual meeting details, just whether a time slot is open or taken.

If this applies to your participants, you'll see a green **"Availability synced"** badge next to their names, and Paceday will filter out any slots where they're busy.

## Coverage indicators

**Green — Availability synced**: Paceday has confirmed access to this person's free/busy data. The slots shown to bookers already account for their existing commitments.

**Yellow — Availability unknown**: Paceday cannot see this person's calendar. The slots shown to bookers are based on your availability only — not theirs. You may still end up with a conflict on their end.

## Why personal email addresses are not checked

If you add a participant with a personal email address — such as a Gmail or Outlook.com account — Paceday will show the yellow "Availability unknown" badge for them.

This is not a technical limitation on Paceday's side. It is a deliberate privacy boundary built into Google's and Microsoft's systems: personal calendars are not accessible to external apps or organisations, even if the person consents in the moment. There is no way for Paceday (or any scheduling tool) to query a personal Gmail or Outlook.com calendar without that person having explicitly set up a specific sharing permission themselves.

The practical upshot: for participants with personal addresses, treat the booked time as a proposal and confirm with them separately before considering it locked in.
