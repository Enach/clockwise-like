title: Collective Scheduling
description: Let bookers see only the slots when every host is free at the same time — perfect for panel interviews, co-led calls, and team demos.

Collective scheduling (sometimes called multi-host scheduling) lets you create a single booking link where the available slots shown to the booker are only the times when **every invited host is free simultaneously**. If even one host has a conflict, that slot disappears from the page.

This is useful any time a meeting needs more than one person from your side — a panel interview, a co-led sales call, a product demo with an engineer present, or any situation where "just send them my link" isn't enough.

## How to add a co-host

1. Open the scheduling link you want to make collective (or create a new one).
2. Scroll to the **Hosts** section and click **Invite co-host**.
3. Enter your co-host's email address and click **Send invite**.

Paceday sends them an email with a link to accept or decline. Until they respond, their status shows as **Pending** on the link's settings page.

## Co-host status badges

On the link's settings page you'll see a badge next to each co-host:

- **Pending** — the invite has been sent but not yet acted on. Paceday cannot check their availability yet, so their calendar is not factored into available slots.
- **Accepted** — the co-host has confirmed. Their calendar is now checked when calculating available slots, and only mutual free times are shown to bookers.
- **Declined** — the co-host has chosen not to participate. Their calendar is not included, and the slot calculation proceeds without them.

## What happens if a co-host declines

If a co-host declines your invite, their availability is removed from the calculation and the link continues to work using the remaining hosts' calendars. You'll see a notification in the Hosts section so you know someone stepped out.

You can re-invite them at any time by clicking **Re-send invite** next to their name, or remove them from the link entirely by clicking the **X** beside their badge.
