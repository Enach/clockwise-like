---
title: What Are Scheduling Links?
description: Scheduling links let other people book time on your calendar without the usual back-and-forth emails.
---

A scheduling link is a public URL you share with someone so they can pick a meeting time that works for both of you — without a single "how about Tuesday?" email.

When someone opens your link, they see a simple page showing only the times you've made available. They pick one, fill in their name and email, and the meeting lands directly on your calendar. You get a confirmation, they get an invite, and nobody had to negotiate.

## How it differs from sending a calendar invite

When you send a traditional calendar invite, you're making a guess about the other person's availability. They accept, decline, or propose a new time — and you go back and forth until something sticks. For busy people this can take days.

With a scheduling link, the other person sees your real availability and self-selects a time that works for them. You do the work of setting it up once, and from then on the booking happens in under a minute.

## When to use a scheduling link

Scheduling links work especially well for:

- **Customer calls** — share a link in an email or on a contact form so prospects can book without waiting for your reply.
- **Interviews** — send candidates a link instead of coordinating with HR to find a mutual slot.
- **Coffee chats** — drop your link in your email signature or on LinkedIn so colleagues can book informal catch-ups on their own.
- **Support calls** — embed the link on a help page so users can book directly from where they're asking for help.
